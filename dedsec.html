About this guide

This page presents high-level operational security (OPSEC) ideas focused on privacy hygiene, threat modeling, and safety. It intentionally does not provide instructions to commit wrongdoing, bypass law enforcement, or hack systems illegally. Follow laws and act ethically.

Core OPSEC Principles

Threat Modeling

Know who or what you're protecting information from. Different threats require different controls.

Least Privilege

Only give accounts or apps the permissions they need. Reduce exposure surface.

Defense in Depth

Multiple layers of protection — physical, device, network, and account layers work together.

Fail-Safe Habits

Make secure choices the default: updates, backups, strong passwords, and multi-factor authentication.

Digital Hygiene

Passwords & Accounts

Use a reputable password manager to create unique, long passwords for each account. Avoid reusing passwords.

Multi-Factor Authentication

Enable MFA everywhere possible — use hardware keys or authenticator apps where supported.

Keep Software Updated

OS, apps, browsers, and firmware updates often patch security bugs. Install updates promptly.

Backups

Keep encrypted backups of important data. Test restores so backups are actually usable.

Device Security

Full Disk Encryption

Encrypt devices using built-in tools (e.g., BitLocker, FileVault) to protect data if a device is lost or stolen.

Secure Boot & TPM

Where available, use secure boot and hardware-backed keys to increase resistance to tampering.

Compartmentalize

Separate sensitive tasks from daily browsing. Use dedicated profiles or devices for high-risk activities.

App Hygiene

Install apps only from trusted sources and review requested permissions critically.

Network & Browsing

Use HTTPS & Privacy-Friendly Browsers

Prefer sites with HTTPS. Consider privacy-respecting browser settings and extensions that block trackers.

VPNs & Proxies — Know the Limits

A reputable VPN can improve privacy on untrusted networks. It does NOT make illegal actions legal or make you immune from investigation. Research providers and their policies.

Secure Messaging

Use end-to-end encrypted messaging apps for private conversations. Understand metadata risks and retention policies.

Physical & Personnel Security

Screen & Boot Security

Use screen locks, shut down devices when not needed, and avoid leaving unlocked devices unattended.

Data Disposal

Wipe drives securely before disposal. Remove accounts from devices when transferring ownership.

Trust & Social Engineering

Validate requests for access. Attackers often use social pressure to bypass technical controls.

Habits & Mindset

Good OPSEC is sustainable — pick a few strong habits and keep them. Review your exposure periodically and practice simple routines (password manager + MFA + updates).

Quick Daily Checklist

• Check critical updates  • Verify backups • Review account MFA status  • Limit public sharing

Legal & Ethical Reminder

This guide is educational and intentionally high-level. It does not provide instructions to engage in illegal behavior, hacking, evading law enforcement, or harming others. Follow laws and use security knowledge for defense, privacy, and legitimate research only.
